The problem

"Learning AI" rarely leads to hiring.

Courses, roadmaps, and tutorials produce people who know about engineering — not people who can do it, or prove it.

01 / PASSIVE

Learning stays passive

Videos, reading, and roadmaps build familiarity, not the muscle memory of building real systems under real constraints.

02 / UNTESTED

No real engineering experience

Most learners finish without ever shipping anything that resembles the work they're being hired to do.

03 / UNVERIFIED

No reliable proof of skill

Portfolios are inconsistent, certificates are weak signals, and self-reported ability can't be trusted.

04 / UNTRUSTED

Employers can't verify ability

Without a measurable signal, employers default to credentials and pedigree — locking out capable beginners.

How it works

A closed loop that converts effort into employability.

Every cycle through the loop moves your skill profile closer to the bar employers actually hire against.

01 — INPUT

Assessment

Your current ability is measured directly against the requirements of real target job roles — not a generic skill quiz.

02 — ANALYZE

Gap analysis

The system identifies exactly which skills are missing, weak, or unverified, based on what the role actually demands.

03 — ASSIGN

Task assignment

You receive structured engineering tasks built specifically to close the gaps identified — never generic exercises.

04 — BUILD

Execution

You build real systems and solutions — the same kind of work expected on the job, not simplified practice problems.

05 — SCORE

Evaluation

Each submission is scored against structured rubrics: correctness, code quality, system design, AI competency, and production readiness.

06 — UPDATE

Skill profile update

Your dynamic skill profile updates in real time, reflecting exactly where you stand against your target role.

07 — REPEAT

Iteration loop

New tasks generate automatically around your remaining weaknesses — the loop continues until you're job-ready.
